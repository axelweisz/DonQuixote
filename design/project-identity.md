# Project Identity — Don Quixote: The Knight of the Sorrowful Face

## Logline
A side-scrolling beat-em-up prototype adapting key episodes from Cervantes' *Don Quixote*, where the player navigates the thin line between heroic delusion and comic reality.

## Prototype Scope
A single playable level recreating the **Battle of the Windmills** episode. Windmills transform between their mundane form and their "true" dragon form (as perceived by Quixote). The player must strike enemies **only in their dragon state** — hitting windmills in their mundane form loses points/health. This single mechanic carries the entire thematic weight of the game.

## Eventual Game Vision
A narrative RPG with dialogue choices, leveraging Cervantes' original text. Passages, speeches and exchanges adapted as in-game dialogue trees. The delusion/reality duality expressed through both visual transformation and branching narrative consequences.

## Visual Style
**Brazilian Cordel literature** — the woodcut/xilogravura aesthetic of northeastern Brazilian popular pamphlets. High contrast black and white with selective color. Bold, rough-edged linework. Flat but expressive. Visually distinctive and culturally meaningful given the history of Cordel adaptations of Don Quixote.

## Tone
Comedic, melancholic and magical realist — true to the spirit of the source material. The humor and the sadness are inseparable. The magic is always ambiguous.

## Target Platform
PC (prototype). Mobile to be considered for full game.

## Purpose
Investor/financing demo. Needs to be visually striking and mechanically clear within minutes of play.
